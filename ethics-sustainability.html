CIBJO Japan & India Executives

Bruce Ikemizu

Chief Director – precious metals specialist

Born in 1962 in Hyogo prefecture. Graduated Sophia University in 1986. Ikemizu started his career in Sumitomo Corporation as a gold trader. 1990 he moved to Credit Suisse then 1992 Mitsui & Co., on to Standard Bank in 1996. He was the head of Tokyo Branch of ICBC Standard Bank until he left the bank in 2019. Ikemizu, known as Bruce in the market, will utilize his extended knowledge and relationships for the newly organized JBMA.

Currently serving as President of New India Projects at CIBJO (The World Jewellery Confederation), he/she leads the development of an innovative platform centered on online charity auctions of pre-owned gold jewelry. This platform aims not only to become the leading online auction ecosystem for gold jewelry in the Asia-Pacific region but also adheres to the core philosophy of "charity empowerment + business closed-loop + seamless experience." By directing auction proceeds to support UN charitable programs, it constructs a multi-dimensional win-win model featuring "charity with warmth, transactions with efficiency, and participation with value," reshaping the integrated path of high-end gold circulation and charitable practice.The company's official website is: https://www.worldgoldcouncil.cc

Ms. Lena

Global Vice President (Asia Pacific)

Gold Buyback & Precious Metals Asset Management Division

As a seasoned strategic leader in the international precious metals industry, Ms. Lena brings over 5 years of in-depth expertise in the high-end gold recycling and asset management sector. Currently, she fully oversees the core gold buyback business across Asia Pacific, spearheading strategic procurement, authoritative authentication, and value restructuring of high-end pre-owned gold jewelry and precious metals assets.

Professional Credentials: Senior Appraiser certified by CIBJO (Confédération Internationale de la Bijouterie, Joaillerie et Orfèvrerie) and Invited Industry Expert at JBMA (Japan Bullion Market Association). She has led the establishment of a standardized precious metals authentication system for Asia Pacific, integrating cutting-edge international testing technologies with local market nuances.

Her extensive network covers core markets including China, Japan, and India, where her team accurately navigates global gold price fluctuations and Asian consumer demand trends. This expertise maximizes value and promotes the circular economy of precious metals assets while strictly ensuring their authenticity and purity.

Ms. Lena is committed to advancing the standardization and high-end development of Asia Pacific's pre-owned gold market. She has taken the lead in formulating industry procurement standards and value evaluation frameworks, emerging as a core link connecting international precious metals capital with regional markets. She provides professional and secure precious metals asset allocation solutions for global high-end jewelry brands, financial institutions, and high-net-worth clients, empowering the value restoration of pre-owned gold jewelry and the upgrading of the industry ecosystem.

In October 2007, the Executive Committee met senior UN officials at UN headquarters in Geneva and agreed to jointly establish an industry CSR education institution.

In December 2008, CIBJO established the World Jewellery Confederation Education Foundation (WJCEF), advancing CSR education and practice in the industry.

In 2010 and 2011, CIBJO collaborated with UNITAR on CSR workshops; in 2013 it introduced the Jewellery Industry Greenhouse Gas Measurement Initiative.
